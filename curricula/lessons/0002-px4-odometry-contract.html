Lesson 0002 · PX4 odometry

The odometry contract: say exactly what you know

Tangible win: after this lesson you can inspect a PX4 VehicleOdometry sample and distinguish its measurement, frame, capture time, uncertainty, and continuity—without confusing “zero” with “unknown.”

1. PX4 does not need to run your SLAM algorithm

A companion computer may use cameras and an IMU to estimate motion. PX4 does not need all of that internal SLAM machinery. It needs a precise message describing the result.

VIO or SLAMestimates motion on the companion computer
→
VehicleOdometrythe typed boundary contract
→
PX4 EKF2validates and fuses selected measurements

The contract is useful beyond SLAM. A swarm logger must preserve enough of it to answer: where did each vehicle think it was, how certain was it, and did its estimate jump?

2. Five kinds of truth travel together

1

Measurement

Position, orientation, velocity, and angular velocity.

2

Frame

The coordinate system that gives the numbers physical meaning.

3

Time

When the sensor information was captured, not merely when PX4 received it.

4

Uncertainty

Variance describes how uncertain the producer believes each measurement is.

5

Continuity

A reset counter announces that the estimate jumped to a new solution.

∅

Availability

NaN means unavailable. A numerical zero is a real measurement.

4. The traps that cause believable failures

FieldTempting mistakeContract meaning
pose_frameAssume every FRD frame rotates with the body.Pose FRD is a fixed local frame with an arbitrary heading offset.
velocity_frameRotate body velocity as though it were already local.BODY_FRD rotates with the vehicle; local NED/FRD does not.
velocityUse zeros when velocity is unavailable.[0,0,0] asserts that the vehicle was measured stationary; use NaNs for unknown.
timestamp_sampleInsert packet-arrival time.It identifies when the underlying measurement was sampled.
*_varianceUse tiny values to look accurate.Smaller variance tells the estimator to trust the measurement more.
reset_counterJump the pose but keep the old count.Increment it when attitude, position, or velocity resets.

6. PX4 source exercise

Goal: predict which parts of a partial external-odometry sample EKF2 can use, then verify that prediction from the call path.

  1. Open VehicleOdometry.msg. Record the allowed pose and velocity frames, invalid markers, quaternion order, units, and reset semantics.
  2. Construct this prediction before reading EKF2: pose is finite in NED, velocity is all NaN, quaternion is identity, and reset_counter changes from 4 to 5. State which measurements should be accepted, which should be ignored, which time should be used, and which reset value should propagate.
  3. Open EKF2.cpp and locate UpdateExtVisionSample(). Follow the finite-value checks, frame switches, quaternion validation, timestamp_sample, and reset_counter. Compare every branch with your prediction.
  4. Run SITL and inspect listener vehicle_odometry -n 1. Annotate the actual pose frame, velocity frame, quaternion, variances, timestamps, and reset counter. Do not assume that the estimator-output topic and external-input topic have the same producer.

Evidence to keep: source locations, packet prediction, observed listener sample, and one sentence distinguishing stationary velocity from unavailable velocity.
