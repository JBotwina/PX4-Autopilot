# Teaching Notes

- The learner previously completed differential equations, but that knowledge is rusty.
- Coordinate-frame and quaternion math is new.
- Prefer concrete PX4 telemetry, top-down geometry, sliders, and prediction exercises before symbolic derivations.
- Interactive, field-by-field message builders are especially effective; reuse them for later PX4 contracts and debugging lessons.
- Introduce one durable idea per lesson and connect it to the `gz_x500_gimbal` issue.
- Every lesson must include a concrete PX4 source-code exercise with a prediction and verification step; passive source reading is not sufficient.
- Prefer focused 20-hour topics that end in bounded PX4 debugging and contribution skills.
- Keep the ultimate swarm-logging goal visible when teaching lower-level PX4 infrastructure.
